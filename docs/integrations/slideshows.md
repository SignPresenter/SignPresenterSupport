# PowerPoint and Keynote Slideshows

<div className="article-intro">

The **Slideshow** playlist type makes it easy to bring an existing PowerPoint or Keynote deck into SignPresenter. Convert your slides to images, upload them all at once, and you have a working signage rotation in minutes.

</div>

## Step 1: Create the Slideshow playlist

1. Sign in to SignPresenter and go to **Step 2: Playlists**.
2. Choose **Horizontal 16:9**.
3. Scroll down and click **Slideshow**. The Edit Playlist screen opens.
4. Name your playlist.

## Step 2: Convert your slides to JPEG

PowerPoint and Keynote slides need to be exported as images:

- [How to convert Keynote slides to images](https://rshankar.com/how-to-convert-keynote-slides-in-to-images/)
- [How to create pictures from PowerPoint slides](https://www.lifewire.com/create-picture-from-powerpoint-slide-4178534)

Both tools let you export your whole deck as a folder of JPEG or PNG files.

## Step 3: Upload the images

1. Back in the SignPresenter Edit Playlist screen, click **Upload**.
2. Highlight all the exported images.
3. Click **Save** to start the upload.
4. After saving, you're returned to the Playlist screen.

## Step 4: Reorder if needed

1. Click your slideshow on the left to reopen the Edit Playlist screen.
2. Drag the slides into the order you want.
3. Click **Save**.

## Step 5: Add to a screen

1. Go to **Step 3: Screens** and select the screen.
2. Add the slideshow to the **Playlists** box, or pick it from the right-hand selection panel.

## Mixing slideshows with other content

You can build a wrapper playlist that interleaves your slideshow with other messages:

1. Create a new playlist with **Step 2: Playlists → Create a New Custom Playlist**.
2. Add your slideshow from **Available Content**.
3. Click the gear icon on the slideshow entry to play only specific slides — for example, slides 1–2.
4. Add other messages between sections.
5. Add the slideshow again with a different range — slides 3–4 — to continue mid-deck.
6. Save.

This is how you'd run "intro slides → announcement → middle slides → weather → final slides" without re-exporting the deck.
